What Is Dandruff & Why It Happens

Dandruff is your scalp shedding skin cells faster than normal—flakes become visible, sometimes oily or yellow. Triggers include excess sebum, sensitivity to Malassezia yeast, weather swings, and product buildup; seborrheic dermatitis is the stubborn cousin with redness and thicker flakes.

  • Dry vs. oily: Both can flake; oil can also feed yeast.
  • Buildup: Heavy pomades & dry shampoo without proper washes irritate the scalp.
  • Skin conditions: Seb derm needs consistent medicated care.

Signs You’re Dealing with Dandruff

  • Persistent flakes despite moisturizing
  • Itch that spikes with sweat/hats
  • Yellowish, oily scale + buildup
  • Red patches around hairline or in beard

Everyday Habits That Help

  • Wash cadence: Medicated 2–3×/week; gentle cleanser in between.
  • Technique: Massage 60–90s; let medicated lather sit 3–5 min.
  • Condition: Lightweight, scalp-safe conditioners prevent rebound dryness.
  • Exfoliate: Soft brush + mild scrub 1×/week—go easy.

Best Products for Treating Dandruff (Barber-Approved)

Rotation that works: pick one medicated shampoo for Mon/Thu and a gentle cleanser on other days. If flakes persist, rotate between two different actives (e.g., ketoconazole + selenium sulfide).

Lifestyle Tips

  • Hydrate, prioritize omega-3s, and keep sugar in check.
  • Manage stress—training, walks, breath work.
  • Stick with your rotation for 3–4 weeks before judging results.

When to See a Dermatologist

If flakes don’t improve after 4–6 weeks—or you see painful redness, thick plaques, or cracking—book a derm. You may need Rx ketoconazole or short-term steroid solutions.

FAQs

Can dandruff cause hair loss?

Indirectly, yes. Chronic inflammation can stress follicles. Calming the scalp supports fuller-looking growth.

Is dandruff contagious?

No—individual factors like oil production and yeast sensitivity drive it.

How often should I wash?

Most clients do best with medicated 2–3×/week plus gentle cleanser in between.

Are natural oils okay?

Lightweights like tea tree or jojoba can help; avoid heavy, pore-clogging oils.

Does dandruff affect beards?

Yep—cleanse, gently exfoliate, and use a light conditioner or toner.

Will a short cut cure it?

No, but it makes management easier while you treat the scalp.
